DocToMind helps clinicians and students to work with medical documents more efficiently by providing:

- document scanning and OCR (e.g., discharge letters, findings, notes)  
- conversion to **editable text**  
- AI-assisted **summaries** (doctor-focused and simple-language versions)  
- extraction of meta-data (patient, dates, diagnoses â€“ if entered by the user)  
- optional **learning cards / Anki TSV export**  
- optional **checklists / â€œnext stepsâ€ suggestions** for educational reflection  

The app is:

- for **educational and informational purposes only**  
- **not** a medical device  
- **not** a substitute for clinical judgment  
- **not** intended for diagnosis or treatment  
- **not** intended to generate binding medical documentation  

Intended Audience / Language Notice:  
DocToMind is designed primarily for medical professionals and medical students in Germany, Austria, and Switzerland. It is provided for educational and clinical support purposes only and does **not** replace clinical judgment, institutional policies, or local guidelines.

ğŸ“š Evidence, AI & Citations

DocToMind uses on-device AI models to **summarise** and **restructure** medical texts.  
To comply with Apple guideline 1.4.1 *Safety: Physical Harm*, the app:

- avoids definitive diagnostic or therapeutic recommendations  
- labels AI-generated output as **non-binding, educational content**  
- encourages verification against original documents and guidelines  
- allows the user to review and edit all generated text  

Any medical information or terminology is based on established clinical usage and literature but **may be incomplete, outdated, or incorrect**. Always verify with original sources and current guidelines.
